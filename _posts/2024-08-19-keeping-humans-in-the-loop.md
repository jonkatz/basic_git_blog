**Premise**

With a caregiver shortage, surely there's some way that AI can augment current efforts. Currently looking at loneliness as the primary problem-to-solve. As a hook, the first prototype records the user's life story for their family, but the intention is that it's therapeutic in the process.

**Weeks so far: User need discovery!**

As many of you know, most product ideas are flawed and it's important to figure out why. So I'm engaging in user research to figure out what's wrong about it and how to fix it. In this short period, I interviewed: 11 caregivers, 4 senior citizens living alone, 3 industry experts and 1 senior living center director. 2 people got their parent to try it!

Learning: The big "duh" take-home is that there is a lot of trepidation around having people talk to a robot. The experience is pretty engaging once people try, but in it's current form, it's not serving a dire need and people are not excited yet to want to get past the initial hurdle.

I think AI companionship is like online dating in that it will become a normal part of life, but is initially met with understandable stigma. In addition to testing moves away from companionship, I'm wondering what messaging/framing, if any, would help get people over that gut reaction.
