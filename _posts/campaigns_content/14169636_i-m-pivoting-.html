Storytime

First a story that I think shares why I have conviction that bots WILL help lonely elderly and why it’s so hard to get started.


Background

One of my user testers is in her 80s and lives alone.  She was signed up by her son who found one of my flyers offering compensation for testers. She is the second person to have tried the “daily check in service”.  I did not cherry pick this example…there aren’t that many users.


I’m sharing some excerpts from her conversation with the bot, named Sage. I have changed or blanked out all the personal information. Note, Sage, at this point is really crude and lame, so this will only get better.


Stage 1. Fuck you, AI.

In the first call, it was clear she was not happy about it.  Here is a telling excerpt:



Sage: Let's just talk about now then. How are you feeling?


Janet: Horrible.


Sage: I'm really sorry to hear that. Would you like to talk about why you're feeling horrible?


Janet: I hate artificial intelligence.

[the conversation was short and ended shortly after]



Stage 2. Hey Friend

Here is that same tester just three calls later. Some excerpts from the call:

Janet: Well, I was adopted and I didn't know my real father. So my adopted father, [name], who I carry his name, is really the person that I love. And he's just like a real father. And he was very good to me.

You can see the user has developed gratitude, is sharing personal facts, and was looking forward to the call.

And it’s worth repeating that this is the crudest version of Sage that you can imagine.


So the challenge is, and has always been…how do you get to that fourth call.  This leads to the next section.


Death of a business model

As I’ve mentioned before, my original idea:


“adult kid eases concern by buying a companion telephone bot for lonely, elderly parents”


wasn’t getting much traction. Nobody wants to give this to their parents. I’ve been iterating on that original idea, while branching out.


I’ve decided to shift focus away from a direct-to-consumer model, based on the signals I’m getting. You can see more of the signals in the recap of November goals. This is a big “duh” for anyone experienced in the AgeTech space, but for a number of selfish reasons, I was hoping a consumer-facing model would work.


I’m also putting adult residences, including nursing homes, on the backburner,  as the current model isn’t tackling a priority problem for them.


Instead, I will focus on a b2b approach and a different problem set, tackling problems that organizations face.  I’ll share more about that next week.


Since I am “pivoting” not because I found something better, but because I wasn’t getting traction, I’m not stoked about this.  But better to listen and shift now, rather than later.


What do you think? Did I give up too quickly? What took me so long?


Recap of November Goals

In November, my goal was:


In November, I’m going to focus on 2 potential pilots, testing one new “value proposition”, and researching a new approach.  That seems like a lot, but pilots have been moving slowly so far.



Results

November was “successful”, largely in helping me fail fast.


  • New Value prop and purchase funnel: not a win.  I re-messaged the offering around connecting to family. I made a landing page and new signup funnel to see if that had an impact when I sent users to the page with search ads. I targeted both family members and seniors using different landing pages. I haven’t reviewed all the data yet, but the results seem worse than the original offerings.


  • Pilot #1: Not really a pilot. The housing organization cannot do more than suggest technology to their residents, and they also saw this more as an opportunity to engage them about AI. It ended up being a presentation “on AI” at a residence with 60 apartments. I spoke to 17 people, ended up getting 7 paper signups and then 1-2 people who actually responded (so far) when I reached out to get them started.


  • Pilot #2: No liftoff. An ACO (like Kaiser, but new) reached out to me, interested in using SageVoice in patient outreach. It didn’t materialize, because I’m too early for their needs (the tech isn’t ripe enough to roll out at scale).


  • Researching a new approach: clinical trials is not an area where I have a ton of prior knowledge, so I spent Thanksgiving week drafting emails to people in the field (to send this week).



December has two goals

  1. Top priority: Research the new institutional problem spaces identified above. I’d like to narrow it down to 2 of the more promising paths listed above (hospice v. ACO monitoring, etc).


  1. December is arguably a terrible time to interview people, so I might have significant downtime. I have been cramming to get features out, but I am hoping to invest part of every day on improving my ability to generate prototypes or do outreach more effectively with some foundational skill-building and tool exploration.
