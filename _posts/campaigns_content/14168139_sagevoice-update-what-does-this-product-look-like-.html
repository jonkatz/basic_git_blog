Looking for lice in all the right places


Stock images are weird and amazing

The actual update is past the pictures…


the result of a search: “excited entrepreneur”



I am too excited to tell you about stock images!


My goal for October is to make this product less scary, help people understand it. For storytelling and demonstration, pictures and videos are really helpful!!  


Without a broad customer base, I’ve been relying a lot on stock images. And it is such a treat!


Stock images have been around for awhile…and they’ve been weird the whole time.  I just didn’t get a chance to play with them before.  Maybe you haven’t either.


What I want to do is just share weird images, but instead here are some take-aways:


  • Stock images are cheap: rather than pay per image, there is a subscription model, where for $10 a month, I can download unlimited licensed stock images to use (non-exclusively) in perpetuity.

  • Coverage is broad.  Below is just the top of the first page of results for “Dad looking for Lice”. There are pages of solid results.

In case you’re wondering…no, my kids do not have lice (at the moment)

  • And they’re now bolstering it with AI images (second row, second from right) and AI similarity detection: the guy in the second row with the mustache prop is in a lice-checking-like position.

  • Once you start looking at stock images, you see the same actors on all your competitors' websites. It’s like when you get a new car and then suddenly you notice that car a lot more. I’ve seen one of my  “grandmas” on other people’s sites fall in love, play catch and be cared for in a nursing home.
